Cliffhanger

As part of my Computer Science A-Level, I have been working on an NEA (Non-Examination Assesment) project. For this project, I have been given full creative control, so I used this as an opportunity to produce a game. This is a very large project that has been developed over approximately 7 months so favourite and has been developed in a way that shows how my ideas can change and how I respond to unforseen obstacles.
This project shows:

  • Conceptualising a game, including gameplay mechanics, level design and user experience.
  • Agile project development, involving the use of cycles to allow change and evaluation throughout development.
  • Designing game features and algorithms.
  • Implementing various programming concepts, such as abstraction and decomposition.
  • Evaluating issues and resolving them accordingly.
  • Responding to feedback and reviewing suggestions.

This project is still in development


The Idea


The inital idea for my game was to create a 2-Dimensional platformer with mechanics inspired by games such as Super Mario Bros. 3, Hollow Knight and Celeste, and narrative and levels/environments inspired by 'The Forgotten Realms' from the lore of D&D. I planned for the game to involve the player traversing 2D levels filled with platforms and obstacles with the aim of travelling upwards to escape from the Hells. The initial idea included a variety of enemies to fight (including boss fights), upgradable player stats and a currency system to purchase new weapons. However, ths idea was far too ambitious and had to be simplified to to constraints in time and skill.

The idea was simplified into a more straightforward 2D platformer. However, I made sure to keep certain features such as various enemy types and abilities with the intent of making the gameplay experience for the player more impressive. The finalised plan was to create a platformer with three levels, three enemy types, 5 abilities to improve player movement and a scoring system with collectibles to find throughout the levels.

The full initial idea can be found in the 'Notes' section

For the first cycle of development, I aimed to create the first level and implement basic player movement. By the end of the cycle, I implemented:

  • The first level
  • Walking and jumping
  • Dashing
  • Player lives
  • Images and sprites (from Kenney)

I started by creating a basic level using different coloured blocks (image 1/2). I had designed the level beforehand on paper and used a tilemap to create it in javascript. I wanted the level to be fairly easy for the player but not too difficult. I tried to use as much space as possible to make the level more complex guide the player through the entire space. To achieve this I used clouds, mushrooms and floating islands to create varying levels of height within the level.
The player's movement is fairly basic in this cycle, they can move and jump and use a dash ability to lauch themselves in any direction. I also used inspiration from Mario for the player having three lives. Gaining more lives will be added in the future.









Cycle 2


For the second cycle of development, I aimed to create a menu system and the first enemy. By the end of the cycle, I implemented:

  • Main menu with framework for future menus
  • Moving enemy with pre-defined path
  • Double jump ability
  • Player collision with enemies

For the menus, I created mockups for each menu in Canva. This allowed me to alter the appearance and structure easily before programming it. For this cycle, I only implemented the home, pause and retry menus (images 1-3) as I have not yet implemented settings options. Images 4-7 show the remaining mockups that are yet to be implemented.
The first enemy was inspired by the Goombas from Mario. However I wanted to make them a more challenging enemy so I gave them the ability to 'burrow'. Every frame there is a random chance for the enemy to enter a burrow state where it is immune to damage, remains still, and pushes a spike upwards (image 9). The spike kills the player and the enemy cannot be moved.
The player can also double jump now. I added this to make movement slightly more fun, this also allows the player to be more strategic with their movement.

For the third cycle of development, I aimed to create checkpoints throughout the level, a shield ability to block damage and spikes areound the map. By the end of the cycle, I implemented:

  • Checkpoints to respawn the player when they die
  • Shield ability which blocks damage
  • Spikes to kill the player
  • Level restarting upon player death

I implemented the checkpoints by creating two new tiles, one for the flag and one for the pole. The tiles have no collider so the player can pass through them. When the player overlaps with a checkpoint, its image is changed from an empty pole to a flag and the coordinates of the checkpoint are stored for when the player next respawns.
For the shield ability, the player can press 's' to make themselves immune to damage before going on a cooldown(unless they fall of the map). The shield icon is displayed in the bottom left corner of the screen and flashes when it is on cooldown.
The spikes were created by making a new tile with the spike image. The tile will kill the player upon collision and are spread around the map to make the level more challenging for the player.









Cycle 4


I am currently working on the fourth cycle of development, I aim to create another enemy type, the second level and a scoring system. By the end of the cycle, I will implement:

  • Ranged enemy which targets the player
  • Second level which is more challenging
  • Scoring system
  • Collectibles
  • Grapple hook to improve player movement (unfinished)

For the second level, I wanted to create a different theme so I used a different set of assets from Kenney. This let me make the level more visually distinct from the previous. Also, I have made this level more challenging than the first by using the environment to guide the player through a longer route with more places to make mistakes than the first.
The second level also introduces the new enemy type. This enemy is fixed and shoots projectiles towards the player. When the player is in range, the enemy angles its shot towards the player and fires with a 1 second delay.
The scoring system awards the player points whenever they kill an enemy or collect a collectible. For the first level, coins are scattered around the map. For the second level, pumpkins award the player points. When the player dies, their score is multiplied by the number of the level they were on and displayed for them.

Further Development


I currently have plans for two more development cycles, implementing a third level, third enemy, high scores database, two more abilities and settings and high scores menus. These will be implemented over the next 2 months and will finalise the development of the game. I will continue to make the game more challenging as the player progresses to ensure that it remains enjoyable and fresh.
